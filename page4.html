Arizona Wind Quintet

Arizona Wind Quintet is the faculty ensemble-in-residence at the University of Arizona Fred Fox School of Music. Formed in 1977, AWQ has cultivated a tradition of excellence in both performance and teaching. The quintet mentors the award-winning Fred Fox Graduate Wind Quintet, premiere student chamber ensemble at the FFSOM. AWQ recently enjoyed a week-long residency at the Universidad Nacional Autónoma de México (UNAM) in Mexico City.

Braiding Sweetgrass Project 

"Listening to the Songs of Plants"
A pre-concert conversation with Robin Wall Kimmerer, ecologist
and Asha Srinivasan, composer

The Braiding Sweetgrass Project is an interdisciplinary effort led by oboist Sara Fraker. Funded by a 2017 Artist Research and Development Grant from the Arizona Commission on the Arts, Fraker commissioned composer Asha Srinivasan to create a piece inspired by Robin Wall Kimmerer’s book, Braiding Sweetgrass: Indigenous Wisdom, Scientific Knowledge and the Teachings of Plants. In this recital, Fraker premiered the compelling new work, “Braiding” for oboe, electronics, and natural sounds.

Kimmerer is a nationally recognized author, professor of environmental and forest biology at the SUNY College of Environmental Science and Forestry, and a member of the Citizen Potawatomi Nation. She speaks frequently on the subject of Native American ecological knowledge and environmental protection. Srinivasan is an award-winning composer and associate professor of music at Lawrence University.

Drawing upon ideas from the emerging field of ecomusicology, The Braiding Sweetgrass Project explores the intersection of environmental sustainability, soundscape, and musical performance. The project brings together students and faculty from wide-ranging disciplines, including music, traditional ecological knowledge and creative writing. It is made possible by grants from the UA Confluencenter for Creative Inquiry, American Indian Studies, Creative Writing Program, College of Fine Arts Fund for Excellence, and Fred Fox School of Music.

Ecomusicology is defined by ethnomusicologist Jeffrey Todd Titon as “the study of music, culture, sound and nature in a period of environmental crisis.” As the worldwide movement for sustainability gains increasing urgency, many artists and scientists are staking new activist territory at the intersections of music, literature, acoustic ecology, and environmental science. As Kimmerer said in her recent address to the United Nations General Assembly, “We will need to enlist artists and poets, storytellers and musicians to remind us of what we love, of what we value, what makes us deeply happy as humans; for the most powerful transformations are motivated by love.”

True Concord Voices & Orchestra 

True Concord Voices & Orchestra performs a wide variety of masterworks and contemporary pieces for chamber choir and various instrumental ensembles. True Concord's 2015 album, Far in the Heavens, features works by Stephen Paulus and was nominated for two Grammy Awards: Best Contemporary Classical Composition (winner) and Best Choral Performance. The ensemble made its Lincoln Center debut in 2015 at Alice Tully Hall.

Bay View Wind Quintet 

The Bay View Wind Quintet performs at the Bay View Music Festival throughout the summer in beautiful Northern Michigan. Every season, BVWQ also mentors a total of four student wind quintets as they pursue intensive study of chamber music. BVWQ was recently featured on the program "In Studio A" at Interlochen Public Radio.
